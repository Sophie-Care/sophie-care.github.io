What is Counselling?


Counselling is a professional, collaborative process where a trained person (a counsellor or therapist) helps a person explore and manage personal, emotional, psychological, or behavioural issues. It provides a safe, confidential space for people to talk about their feelings, thoughts, and challenges without judgement.

  • Confidentiality: What you share stays private (within legal and ethical limits).
  • Non-judgemental support: The counsellor offers empathy, understanding, and respect.
  • Goal-oriented: It may help you improve mental health, resolve conflict, make decisions, or cope with difficult life events.
  • Voluntary participation: The process is typically based on the clientâ€™s willingness and readiness to engage.

Common Issues Addressed in Counselling:

  • Anxiety or depression
  • Relationship issues
  • Stress and burnout
  • Grief and loss
  • Self-esteem or identity issues
  • Trauma or abuse
  • Life transitions (e.g., divorce, job change)
